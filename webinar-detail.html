e-Invoicing — Industry Focus: Healthcare

Overview

00:00 – 04:00

This course examines how Malaysia's e-Invoicing mandate applies to the healthcare sector. It addresses the unique billing complexities involving hospitals, insurance companies, patients, and third-party consultants, and how each transaction type must be handled to achieve full compliance.

Key Concepts

Healthcare Provider & Insurance Transactions 04:00 – 07:00

When payment flows from insurer to provider, the e-Invoice must reflect the actual payer rather than the patient. Correctly identifying the "buyer" in each billing scenario is fundamental to compliance when dual billing is involved.

Refundable & Non-Refundable Deposits 07:00 – 10:00

Refundable deposits are not subject to e-Invoicing at collection — only upon forfeiture or conversion. Non-refundable deposits require an e-Invoice at the point of collection. Correct classification prevents compliance gaps.

Third-Party Consultants & Space Rentals 10:00 – 16:00

Hospitals engaging external specialists must issue separate e-Invoices for consultant services. Space rental arrangements within hospital premises and staff medical expense reimbursements each carry distinct e-Invoicing obligations that must be documented accurately.

Conclusion

16:00 – 19:00

Proper e-Invoicing implementation in healthcare requires understanding each transaction type and its corresponding obligations. Consistent classification and documentation are essential for meeting LHDN requirements while preserving operational efficiency across all billing scenarios.

Description

This course provides an in-depth understanding of the implementation of e-invoicing in Malaysia's healthcare industry. It covers key billing scenarios such as dual billing for insurance companies and patients, handling of proforma bills for insurance claims, invoicing for consultant services, and managing service contracts between hospitals and doctors.

The course also explores the requirements for issuing e-invoices for non-refundable admission deposits, staff medical expenses, and space rentals within hospital premises. By the end of this course, you will understand how e-invoicing impacts billing processes in healthcare.

  • e-Invoicing Treatment for Refundable & Non-Refundable Deposit
  • e-Invoicing Treatment for Healthcare Services and Third-Party Consultants
  • Other e-Invoicing Treatments in Healthcare Industry
